Background

My prior research was focused on human-centered, data-driven research, which lies at the intersection of trustworthy and explainable AI. It leverages publicly available survey data, including individuals‚Äô demographic, habit, economic, social, family, and health histories, to develop simple and cost-effective ML models for predicting coronary and subjective cognitive conditions, making health-related facilities more affordable to society. In doing so, I address challenges such as generative modeling and guided feature selection, while ensuring model interpretability to provide person-specific prevention strategies and risk factor analysis. Beyond survey-based studies, I have worked on addressing societal challenges, such as mitigating racial bias in AI models for skin cancer prediction to ensure AI tools perform equitably for all ethnic groups in society, EEG-based brain signal analysis for emotion recognition and consumer preference prediction with interpretability to highlight important brain regions and electrodes for providing more optimized analysis of human brain signals, and using open LLMs like GPT-2 for disease management by converting tabular data into text and employing text paraphrasing to address class imbalance for the utilization of short clinical structured data for language modeling. I have also explored LLM reasoning through a gradient-based interpretability method to identify which clinical words matter most for the model‚Äôs decisions. I am motivated to conduct these works because they have the potential to directly improve human-centric outcomes, enable fairer and more personalized interventions, and inform decision-making with transparent and trustworthy AI tools for enhancing AI-based trust in society. My expertise spans diverse data domains, including text, structured data, signals, and images, as well as robust modeling, synthetic data generation (text, tabular, and image), prediction bias mitigation, and predictive optimization. With publications in leading Q1 journals and conferences, I bring experience in independently identifying research gaps, translating ideas into real-world applications, and driving interdisciplinary collaborations toward impactful, trustworthy, and useful AI solutions.
